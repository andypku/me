I am a Master's student in political science at Duke University. Prior to Duke, I earned my Bachelor's degree in political science and economics at Peking University. My research interests lie in authoritarian politics (with a regional focus on China) and computational methods. In particular, I am working on state-society interaction, digital authoritarianism, political selection, causal learning, and deep learning for social science.
